Astrophotography

Snapshots of the night sky and its many mysteries.

about this project

These photographs were captured using various telescopes at two different observatories: the Guilford College Cline Observatory in Greensboro, NC and the West Optical Observatory at the Pisgah Astronomical Research Institute (PARI) in Western NC. I began learning to use telescopes and observational equipment in the spring of 2018, through an astronomy course at Guilford College. This sparked an interest in both astrophysics research and astrophotography, which led to several further projects, including an artistic exploraiton and planetary photography and a scientific research project on variable stars.

Many thanks are due to those who gave me the opportunity to photograph the stars, and to my recurring lab partner, for the many nights spent together in the cold exposing images. Featured here are several of the best color-composite images I have been able to capture.

Perspectives

Analog processes and surreal compositions.

about this project

These images are part of an ongoing series exploring the technique of combination printing: a darkroom process that involves printing several images on one page. The technique was pioneered by Jerry Uelsmann, and I embraced it as a way to enjoy the photographic process regardless of its efficiency. Each of these images involves in some way the relationship between the sky and the ground, the otherwordly and the worldly.

All of the images here are digital scans of 11x14 or 8x10 silver gelatin prints, typically made from several enlargers projecting images from 120mm film. All film and paper were developed in Caffenol, a coffee-based alternative to traditional developers like Rodinal.
