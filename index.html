About me

Hello! I am an undergraduate student currently studying physics at Grinnell College in Grinnell, IA. There I work as mentor at the Data Analysis and Social Inquiry Lab and participate in research into dwarf galaxy evolution with the Physics Department in collaboration with the N-Body Shop. I also work with the University of Florida Astronomy Department and the SIMBA simulation team to study massive quiescent galaxies. I am also an avid photographer and enjoy exploring art, design, architecture, and urban planning.

This personal website is intended to showcase both astrophysics research projects and other artistic/academic endeavors.

Research


My current research interests lie broadly in galaxy formation and evolution, particularly in utilizing data-intensive and computational methods. In the past, I have worked on measuring the light-curves of variable stars and on astronomical imaging techniques.

Photography


Photography has been an important hobby of mine for years, and is what first got me looking to the stars. This page features some of my favorite work in astrophotography and in the darkroom.
